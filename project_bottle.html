Simulation-Based Prevention of Bottle Tipping in Automated Production Lines

Project for:
Role FEA Analyst
Simulation Type Discrete Element Method (DEM) and Finite Element Method (FEM) Simulation
Software Used
ANSYS Rocky

Objective

To analyze and address the issue of bottle tipping within the production line of a large-scale manufacturing facility. The focus was on identifying primary causes of instability and implementing optimized process parameters to ensure uninterrupted, efficient bottling operations.

Contribution

  • Developed and simulated a detailed virtual model of the bottling line for a major P&G product using advanced DEM-FEM coupling techniques.
  • Investigated the bottling process from initial filling through to end-of-line packaging to map out key points of instability and potential bottling failures.
  • Scaled simulation studies from a single-bottle analysis to a complex, multi-bottle configuration of up to 40 bottles, accurately replicating real-world production scenarios.
  • Analyzed the effects of conveyor speeds, bottle spacing, material properties, and line geometry to identify and mitigate tipping risks.
  • Proposed data-driven design modifications and operational improvements, including adjustments to conveyor settings and bottle handling protocols, to minimize the occurrence of bottle tipping.

Results

Achieved a 70% reduction in bottle tipping and a 15% increase in production throughput by developing DEM-FEM simulations that identified instability causes and optimized conveyor and handling parameters for P&Gâ€™s automated bottling line.

Key Achievements:

  • Resolved root causes of bottle tipping, reducing incidents by over 70%.
  • Boosted line throughput by approximately 15%.
  • Cut unplanned stoppages.
  • Delivered simulation insights and documentation supporting ongoing process optimization.
