Reducing Grommet Formation in Non-Adhesive Bonding of Non-Woven Fabric

Project for:
Role FEA Analyst
Simulation Type Structural Simulation,

Arbitrary Lagrangian-Eulerian (ALE) Simulation,

and Multiphysics Simulation
Software Used
LS-DYNA Abaqus Fortran Python

Objective

To predict and reduce grommet formation during high-speed, non-adhesive bonding of non-woven fabric by optimizing the nub-anvil interaction, utilizing advanced simulation techniques and material modeling. The project included the development of a novel polymer material model to accurately simulate phase transitions under rapid compression.

Contribution

  • Developed FEA models in LS-DYNA and Abaqus to simulate grommet formation in high-speed polymer bonding.
  • Created a custom material model capturing polymer phase transitions and deformation under ultra-fast processes.
  • Applied ALE methods to model interactions between the anvil, polymer nubs, and fabric, minimizing numerical instabilities.
  • Identified causes of excessive grommet formation and proposed nub and anvil design improvements, validated in pilot trials.
  • Collaborated with engineering teams to integrate simulation insights, accelerating design iterations and improving quality.

Results

Reduced grommet formation by over 60% in high-speed non-adhesive bonding of non-woven fabrics by leveraging ALE-based FEA and custom polymer modeling, improving product quality and enabling repeatable, simulation-driven process optimization at P&G.

Key Achievements:

  • Reduced grommet formation over 60% in pilot production through simulation-guided design optimization.
  • Created a state-of-the-art, user-defined material model for polymers.
  • Enhanced product quality and consistency, resulting in fewer bonding defects and stronger fabric integrity.
  • Developed a repeatable simulation and testing protocol for efficient troubleshooting and process improvements across P&G sites.
