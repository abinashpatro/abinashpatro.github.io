Wave Impact on Structure

Project for:
Role Doctoral Researcher
Simulation Type Fluid-Structure Interaction (FSI) Simulation
Software Used
LS-DYNA ANSYS FORTRAN

Objective

To simulate wave impact on a solid structure using advanced SPH techniques coupled with multiphysics simulation, developing a numerical framework that captures fluid-structure interaction under dynamic wave loading. This project also served as an instructional tutorial for the Multiphysics course in the Mechanical Engineering department.

Contribution

  • Developed SPH models within LS-DYNA to simulate free-surface water flow and wave impact phenomena on rigid and elastic structures, accurately replicating complex hydrodynamic behavior.
  • Captured key flow features such as wave propagation, breaking waves, splash-up, and vortex formation using weakly compressible SPH formulations.
  • Integrated fluid-structure interaction capabilities to analyze dynamic structural responses under wave impact loading, considering both hydrodynamic forces and structural deformation.
  • Leveraged simulation insights to understand multiphysics coupling effects, establishing a validated computational approach applicable to manufacturing process challenges involving fluid-solid interactions.

Results

Developed an SPH-based LS-DYNA framework to simulate wave-structure interactions, accurately capturing dynamic fluid forces and structural responses, and providing a validated multiphysics tool for process and design optimization.

Key Achievements:

  • Replicated experimental wave impact behavior, capturing force profiles and fluid flow patterns in LS-DYNA.
  • Delivered a validated framework to extend multiphysics analyses to processes like non-adhesive bonding.
  • Supported improved process design by clarifying fluid-induced forces on structural responses.
