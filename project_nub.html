Progressive Damage Simulation of Fiber-Reinforced Composites

Advanced Finite Element Analysis for Material Failure Prediction

Project for:
Role Simulation Engineer / Researcher
Simulation Type Nonlinear Static and Progressive Damage FEA
Software Used
LS-DYNA Python

Objective

To predict and analyze fatigue failure in tool nubs used for non-adhesive bonding of non-woven fabric during the manufacturing cycle, with the aim of redesigning the nub geometry to significantly extend component lifespan.

Contribution

  • Developed a comprehensive fatigue failure simulation model leveraging LS-DYNA to assess the performance and failure modes of tool nubs under cyclic loading conditions.
  • Analyzed stress distribution, fatigue life, and fracture initiation sites to identify critical factors limiting nub durability.
  • Collaborated closely with the manufacturing team to redesign nub geometry, integrating simulation-driven insights to enhance mechanical strength and fatigue resistance.
  • Proposed material adjustments and surface treatment options based on simulation results to further improve tool longevity.

Results

Extended tool nub lifespan by over 40% and reduced maintenance downtime by applying LS-DYNA fatigue simulations to guide geometry redesign and material optimizations in P&G’s non-adhesive bonding process.

Key Achievements:

  • Increased tool nub lifespan by over 40% through design modifications.
  • Reduced maintenance and tool replacement frequency, boosting uptime and cutting costs.
  • Delivered documented fatigue models and redesign protocols to support future tool improvements.
