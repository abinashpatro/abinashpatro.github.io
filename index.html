I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Cincinnati with expertise in computational mechanics, finite element analysis (FEA), and multiphysics simulations. Over the past several years, I have developed advanced material models, large-scale simulation workflows, and high-performance computing (HPC) frameworks to solve complex problems in composites, polymers, adhesives, and manufacturing systems.

My professional experience spans collaborations with Procter & Gamble's Digital Accelerator, Siemens Simulation Center, and academic research groups, where I have designed and executed structural, thermal, and dynamic simulations that improved product performance, reduced defects, and accelerated innovation.

With a strong background in LS-DYNA, ANSYS, Abaqus, COMSOL, and Python/C++/FORTRAN programming, I bring a blend of simulation expertise, material science knowledge, and automation skills to tackle challenging engineering problems. My research has been published in leading journals, focusing on progressive damage modeling, fatigue prediction, and mesh regularization in composites.
